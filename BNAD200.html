Course Description
Career Management in Business is designed to inform students about career planning and management interventions and to accommodate students at different levels of career decidedness. Career management includes self-assessment of values, interests, and skills, exploration of career options, and learning essential job search skills. This course prepares students to take ownership of their career journey through a series of skill-building modules and real-world experiences.
✎
Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this course, you will be able to:
  1. Understand how your personal characteristics (e.g. values, interests, and skills) influence career management through self-assessment and goal setting.
  2. Identify appropriate academic majors, training, and/or occupational alternatives in relation to values, personality, interests, and skills.
  3. Use informational resources to explore academic majors and career options.
  4. Develop a high-level understanding of the National Association of Colleges & Employer's Career Readiness Competencies
✎
Course Competencies
Module 1: Self-Assessment & Goal Setting
  • Evaluate personal priorities, interests, and skills
  • Understand research strategies related to business majors/sectors
Module 2: Building Your Professional Development Foundation
  • Create a resume and cover letter
  • Understand and practice interview skills
Module 3: Career Research & Exploration
  • Conduct employer and industry research
  • Practice professionalism and digital presence (LinkedIn, etc.)
Module 4: Experiential Learning
  • Apply communication and teamwork skills through a business case study
  • Identify extracurricular and co-curricular leadership opportunities

Assignment: Sparkhire Virtual Interview

Background: Companies are increasingly using video assessments as a 1st round interview instead of in-person interviews.

Your assignment: Record your answer to 2 common interview questions as if it were for an internship application.

Questions:

  • Tell me about yourself (your elevator pitch)
  • Tell me about a time you overcame a significant obstacle (use STAR method)
Assignment: Informational Interview
The purpose of this assignment is to understand and complete all steps required to conduct an informational interview successfully. You will identify potential interviewees, reach out and schedule an interview, conduct company research to prepare for your interview, conduct the interview, and reflect on the conversation.
Case Competition
As a team, you will analyze a business situation and recommend a solution through a visual aid presentation. Your group is role playing as the Eller Client Consulting Company, and you are paid to help businesses improve sales and increase revenue.
